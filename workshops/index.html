MATLAB Workshop

MATLAB is soon becoming an important asset to every Engineering student. This workshop will give a detailed explanation on the various facets of MATLAB right from the very basics. Handled by thorough professionals, this workshop will be extremely beneficial for students of all years.

Internet of Things Workshop

Internet of Things is the new in-thing in the field of Engineering. IoT is paving the way for the new world where almost every day-to-day device is automated. Through this workshop, students will get a introduction as to how one can delve into the world of IoT and also some much needed hands-on experience.

Robotics and Automation

Went crazy over movies like "Enthiran" and "I, Robot"? Every robot that is built is a masterpiece, a mini-world of sensors and actuators and what not. Robots are expected to run the future. Ever wished you could test your flair for it? Here's your chance. Buckle up and be at SSN for the Robotics workshop on the 9th of September.

Career Guidance

Are you stuck between a rock and a hard place? The world of engineering and technology is vast and potentially confusing without the right guidance. IoT Hackathon


Description:­ Participants are to build a prototype of an application on the theme ”Internet of Things” with the provided components and any additional components they may choose to bring. Participation is subject to registration with a proposal and limited to a fixed number of teams (10-­15).

Team Size:­­​ Maximum of 3 people.

Prize money:­­​ ₹6000, ₹3000, ₹1500 for first, second and third place respectively.

Proposed Venue:­­​ Library

­​

Rules:­

  1. Each team may consist of up to 3 members.
  2. There is no separate registration fee of the event,other than general INVENTE registration fee.
  3. The teams will be provided with an ESP8266 WiFi module and one of either an Arduino or an Intel Galileo board, passive components, a regulated power supply, CRO, function generator and basic soldering facilities.
  4. Each team must submit a proposal for an application that they will be able to implement within the given timeframe.
  5. The teams will be required to bring any additional components required, outside the given list.
  6. The top 10­15 teams will be shortlisted to appear in the hackathon.
  7. The teams will be asked to implement their proposal with a few changes within the given timeframe.
  8. Points will be awarded for speed and robustness of the implementation.
  9. The team judged to be best according to the criteria specified will be named the winner, with runner­up and second runner­up also being judged.
